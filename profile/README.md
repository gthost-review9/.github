## Honest Drawbacks: Where GTHost Falls Short

### Unmanaged by Default

This is the biggest filter. GTHost's servers are unmanaged by default, requiring in-house sysadmin skills. If you're not comfortable with SSH, Linux server administration, and managing your own stack, you'll need to either hire someone or look at a managed hosting provider instead.

GTHost is built for developers and technical teams. It is not a good fit for people who want a control panel dashboard that handles everything for them automatically.

### No Money-Back Guarantee

There is no money-back guarantee, which is not unusual for dedicated server providers — but it's worth stating plainly. The trial period compensates for this, but once you're on a monthly plan, refunds aren't on the table if you change your mind.

### Live Chat Availability Can Be Inconsistent

Live chat was unavailable when tested, though email queries resulted in almost instantaneous responses. For support emergencies, email and phone work well, but if you specifically want live chat, the experience may vary.

### Limited Asian Coverage

GTHost's footprint is strong in North America and Europe. If your primary audience is in Southeast Asia or East Asia, you'll likely need a CDN on top of GTHost's infrastructure to get acceptable latency for those users.
